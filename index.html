By Belinda Addinall

In today's busy world many of us suffer minor ailments, headache, stress, sore feet and even sleep deprivation. These are very easily fixed with a pill or two, but at what cost?

There are alternatives to chemicals to remedy most common and non life threatening ailments. Medication prescribed by a doctor should never be left for alternative remedies. With any serious condition always follow your physician’s advice and never use anything without consulting him first. The uses of essential oils has been around for centuries.

History has suggested that the Egyptians were amongst the first people to extensively make use of herbs and included their use in religion, beauty as well as medicinal purposes. Essential oils and resins were also extensively used in the embalming process. The Egyptians via the Mesopotamians had access to distillation pots that have been found at Tepe Gawra dating back about 3,500 BC.

The medicinal wisdom of the Egyptians were taken over and used by the ancient Greeks - and the most well-known physician of that time - Hippocrates (c.460 - 377 BC) was also a firm believer of treating the patient holistically and included essential massage as a treatment. The Persian physician Avicenna (980 - 1,037 AD) that has being credited with perfecting the distillation process of essential oils. Since the late 1970 and early 80s the use of essential oils and aromatherapy has become a major part of alternative and holistic health systems, and has a huge following across the world. The term aromatherapy as we know it today was first used in 1937 by the French chemist and perfumer R. M. Gattefosse. He was not a believer of the natural health movement but was interested in the properties that essential oils exhibited. In 1910 he burnt his hand badly in his laboratory and being the first available compound handy, treated his badly burnt hand with pure undiluted lavender oil, which not only immediately eased the pain, but helped heal the hand without any sign of infection or scar. He also found that minute amounts of essential oils are absorbed by the body and interact with the body chemistry.

That being said never use oils you're not familiar with or that you have not gotten from a professional therapist. Please be aware that all oils except for Lavender and Tea tree oils, should be used diluted.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

  • Can I drink essential oils?
  • Considering that it takes 4000(1814.37kg) pounds of rose petals to make 1(453grams) pound of essential oil, the concentration should tell us that a safe dose is unlikely! Some oils are toxic, so No!
  • Have your products been tested on animals?
  • Absolutely Not! In addition, all our skin and hair care products contain only Plant (herb, flower etc) derived ingredients
  • Why is quality important with essential oils?
  • To obtain acceptable results with aromatherapy it's vital to use only the very finest quality pure essential oils. Inferior quality essential oils deliver unpredictable results meaning ultimately you would have wasted your money and risked your health. Using poor quality oils ou may have an allergic reaction to the oil and experience a skin rash, or other side effects.
  • What is the difference between Aromatherapy oils and Essential oils?
  • Aromatherapy Oil is usually a 2% dilution of an essential oil and 98% almond or grape seed oil. Which is bad value for money, as it’s just a form of massage oil sold in 10ml bottles. Nut oils should be avoided as they are high risk as allergens. Essential oils are undiluted products that have been extracted from flowers, herbs, leaves, grasses, roots, woods, barks, spices, fruits, or gum. Of which most should not be used without carrier oil.
  • How should my Essential Oils be stored?
  • Store oils always away from direct sunlight. Store them in dark bottles and if possible in a fridge that set between 5 and 10 degrees Celsius.
  • How long do essential oils last?
  • Except for a few exceptions, all steam distilled essential oils have a shelf life of at least 2 years, and even more when stored properly. Tea Tree, Pine and Fir oils are the above mentioned exceptions. They have a shelf life of 12 to 18 months, due to certain components in their natural chemical composition.
  • Can I use Essential Oils safely with other products?
  • Yes Essential oils need to be diluted, to be used safely. I always recommend a professional mixes your products or you do it under their advice and guidance.
  • Can I be allergic to an Essential Oil?
  • If you are allergic to Lemons its safe to assume you will be allergic to the Lemon Essential Oil. We all have allergies and thus test on a small space first before using any oil. Avoid carrier oils that are known allergens.
  • Can I use cooking Olive Oil?
  • High quality Olive Oil should be used and unfortunately most Cooking oils are not the highest quality. The healthy olive oils have been made by the cold pressed method. Olive Oil should be bought from an Essential Oil Company to be guaranteed of the quality.
  • Can I steam with Essential oils?
  • Yes you can, people with asthma should however not use this treatment. This is very effective for upper respiratory infections. Please always remember to not use any product without your doctor's consent if you’re under treatment.
